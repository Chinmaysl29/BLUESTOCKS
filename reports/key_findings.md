# EDA Key Findings

1. SBI AMC dominates industry AUM.
   SBI Mutual Fund has the highest AUM in the dataset, reaching 1,250,000 crore on 2025-12-31, and also leads cumulative AUM across reported periods.

2. SIP inflows peaked in Dec 2025.
   Monthly SIP inflows reached 31,002 crore in 2025-12, the highest value in the SIP inflow dataset.

3. Debt/Liquid funds attracted the highest inflows; equity-oriented categories were the next major contributor.
   The data does not support the statement that equity funds attracted the highest inflows overall. Liquid funds alone recorded 451,275 crore, and broad Debt categories totaled 517,200 crore versus 376,171 crore for equity-oriented categories.

4. Large-cap funds showed stable growth.
   Large Cap NAVs showed comparatively stable movement within equity schemes, with lower daily return volatility than most other equity sub-categories.

5. Technology and BFSI dominate holdings.
   Banking and IT have the highest summed portfolio weights, with Banking at 652.26 and IT at 455.47.

6. Younger investors contribute more activity.
   The 26-35 age group has the highest transaction count at 13,463, followed by 36-45 at 8,146.

7. Metro cities contribute majority investments.
   T30 cities account for the majority of investment transactions, with 21,719 transactions versus 11,059 from B30 cities.

8. Expense ratios remain below 2%.
   All 40 schemes have expense ratios below 2%, with a maximum expense ratio of 1.64%.

9. Strong NAV correlation exists among top funds.
   The selected top 10 schemes show mostly positive NAV correlations, with an average off-diagonal correlation of 0.741.

10. Industry folio count nearly doubled from 2022 to 2025.
    Total folios increased from 13.26 crore in 2022-01 to 26.12 crore in 2025-12, a 1.97x increase.
